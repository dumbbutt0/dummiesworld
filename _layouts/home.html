── // about me ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

I'm a compute-constrained builder figuring out how far you can push AI without a frontier-model budget. The work is about systems, not size: wiring a model into memory, tools, agents, knowledge graphs, and governance so a small setup punches above its weight.

I'm especially interested in retrieval & memory, local-first knowledge graphs, agent loops, evaluation, and the privacy boundary between what a system graphs locally and what it ever shares. I build the tooling around all of it — a self-maintaining wiki, a live graph "lens", and an event-driven autonomy loop.

This blog is the logbook: notes on building AI systems on a budget, the architecture decisions behind them, and what actually works when the model stays the same but the system gets smarter.
